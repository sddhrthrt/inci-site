There is a broad agreement that violence in all its forms has gone up in both general and specific terms. Of late, crimes against women are showing an upward trend, in India in general. The gruesome case of Nirbhaya had paved way for many reformative amendments to existing laws and yet, the onslaught goes on.
Are legal reforms solely capable of changing the scenario? Can it set right the gender balance? What can we as the civil society do to root out this social evil?
Decades of struggle and numerous international conventions and constitutional provisions, the answers are not clear, despite pro-active legislation.
One basic cause for this could be attributed to the lack of legal awareness as well as its social perspective, and the intricacies involved in the ameliorating the situation.
Sensing the need of the hour, Incident iCare 2014, in association with NITK-IEEE student chapter, take pride in hosting a talk and panel discussion on Crime against Women: An Indian Perspective, to be graced by dignitaries from the society.
During the programme, the dignitaries would also be releasing NITK-IEEE's The Jagrit Bachpan Project, an in-house developed mobile and web application platform aimed at streamlining and fast-tracking child abuse reporting, based on the philosophy that the easier it is to do something and make a change for the better, the more the chances that one would indeed try.
Venue : ISTE Seminar Hall

I-Dea is a group event, to crack up your mind and come up with some innovative out-of-box solutions to various present world problems. Brain storm, come up with ideas, may be working solutions, anything and win exciting prizes!

Problem statements:

  1. Given that all of us have heard of ways to be more socially conscious or nature friendly, why is that no one follows them? What are the reasons for the existing gap between people intentions and their actual behavior when it comes to environmental issues? A lot of NGOs work with this objective of creating an environment friendly world. But they donâ€™t seem to motivate the people enough to change their way of living. So how could we bring about a change in attitude of the socially unconcerned people? Can marketing tools be of any use in erasing this gap and motivating consumers to convert their intentions into actions?
  2. Today NGOs are facing one major problem. Though they have a large number of volunteers who wish to work, but there are only very few people who actively participate and are ready to take an initiative. Their lethargy/busy schedule takes over the interest in giving their contribution to the society. Thus the NGOs find difficulties in keeping them positively engaged/motivated for the entire period of time. Come up with a detailed plan to overcome this issue.
  3. We know how a Company (for-profit organization) is performing in the market by observing the profits and losses made by the company. But what about the NGOs? Quantifying performance and measuring results are no longer the sole domain of for-profit enterprises. Today, many NGOs find themselves unable to answer the donors who expect to see proper accounts to show how their money was spent and what that spending achieved. And quantifying the performance also plays a crucial role in publicizing/expanding the NGO. Yet there has been a great difficulty to establish a set of hard-and-fast metrics to measure social performance. How should this issue be handled?
  4. Everyday we read about rape cases in the newspapers. Some are blown up, known to everyone, and some just die the silent death. So even after so many protests and so many laws in place, still women safety is a big issue? Any women to travel alone at night fears. Eve-teasing happens in broad daylight. So what do you think can be done to increase protection for women? What can be done so that women will be confident to wander out without fear?
